Projects

IEEE Micromouse

This project was developed as a part of IEEE's micromouse team. A micro mouse is a robot designed to navigate a maze. We created a PCB using EasyEDA and picked components based on specific parameters to achieve this. We used a Teensy 4.1 microcontroller as it's more powerful and smaller than the Arduino Nano. Regarding algorithms, we utilized A*, a variant of Dijkstra's algorithm but added a new heuristic to ensure the mouse was heading in the correct direction, resulting in less trial-and-error. I worked on Interfacing the Time-Of-Flight sensors via I2C and the shift register. Programming was done in C++ using Arduino's Abstraction Layer. We competed at New York Institute of Technology.

Time to talk from a less technical perspective. Every year, Rutgers Programming Association holds a Bed Wars competition. Teams of 3 will race, where two members drag a bed with one person sitting on it. In addition to the speed, teams are judged by their uniforms and the bed. Beforehand, the process consisted of the judges starting a stopwatch, stopping the stopwatch, inputting the data into a spreadsheet, and so on. We put that task into the convenient form factor of a program. It implements a stopwatch, records the data, automatically rewards points where appropriate, and calculates based on the judge's uniform, bed, and donation ratings. Finally, it will print out the rankings on a new .txt file.

Music Listening Quiz Tool

I developed this Listening Quiz tool when I was taking music history. In this class, we had what was called Listening Quizzes. Essentially, the professor would randomly play an excerpt of music, and the student would write the piece's composer, name, period, year, and style of music. I wanted to gain a better understanding of Object Oriented Programming and Java.Util.Scanner, so I developed this tool that would randomly pick one of the pieces provided by the user, then play it randomly for 30-60 seconds. Afterward, it will prompt the user to input the information needed.
